About Us

Rice is the most important crop in the world, with nearly half of the human population relying on it for daily sustenance (Singh et al., 2016) due to its nutritional properties and low cost (Hirzel et al.,2020). Rice accounts for approximately 95% of total food grain consumption (Sarma, 1999). It provides about 21% of the world's population's daily caloric intake (Parameswari et al.,2014). It is grown on approximately 163 million ha worldwide, of which 145 million ha are in Asia. Asia consumes 90% of the rice that is produced worldwide (Verma et al.,2018). Bangladesh has nearly achieved food self-sufficiency, particularly in rice production (Mannaf and Uddin, 2012). Rice is using up around 75% of the country’s agricultural land’s and contributing 28% of its GDP (Chowhan et al., 2017).
